Abstract

Precise Text-to-Image (T2I) generation has achieved great success but is hindered by the limited relational reasoning of static text encoders and the error accumulation in open-loop sampling. Without real-time feedback, initial semantic ambiguities during the Ordinary Differential Equation trajectory inevitably escalate into stochastic deviations from spatial constraints. To bridge this gap, we introduce AFS-Search (Agentic Flow Steering and Parallel Rollout Search), a training-free closed-loop framework built upon FLUX.1-dev. AFS-Search incorporates a training-free closed-loop parallel rollout search and flow steering mechanism, which leverages a Vision-Language Model (VLM) as a semantic critic to diagnose intermediate latents and dynamically steer the velocity field via precise spatial grounding. Complementarily, we formulate T2I generation as a sequential decision-making process, exploring multiple trajectories through lookahead simulations and selecting the optimal path based on VLM-guided rewards. Further, we provide AFS-Search-Pro for higher performance and AFS-Search-Fast for quicker generation. Experimental results show that our AFS-Search-Pro greatly boosts the performance of the original FLUX.1-dev, achieving state-of-the-art results across three different benchmarks. Meanwhile, AFS-Search-Fast also significantly enhances performance while maintaining fast generation speed.

Methodology

Figure 3: The pipeline operates in four phases: Prompt Optimization, Initial Structure Generation, Parallel Rollout Search, and Global Feedback.

Unlike traditional open-loop sampling where initial semantic ambiguities accumulate irreversibly, AFS-Search reformulates text-to-image generation as a navigable, closed-loop decision-making process. The framework tackles spatial grounding through two core components:

1. Parallel Rollout Search (PRS)

At a critical decision point \( t_{split} \) (e.g., 60% of total steps), a VLM acts as a semantic critic to diagnose defects. Based on this diagnosis, we construct a search tree to explore alternative futures:

  • Baseline Branch: Continues the standard ODE trajectory without intervention.
  • Exploration Branch: Injects controlled Gaussian noise \( \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I) \) to escape local semantic optima and resample global layouts.
  • Corrective Branch: Activates Agentic Flow Steering (AFS) to actively rectify specific semantic defects.

Each branch undergoes a short-horizon simulation. The VLM critic then evaluates the previews using a multi-dimensional scoring mechanism (Prompt Adherence, Relational Logic, and Visual Integrity) to select the optimal trajectory.

2. Agentic Flow Steering (AFS)

Instead of passively shifting latent states, AFS functions as an active optimal controller by formulating generative intervention as an energy-minimization problem over the velocity field:

  • Linear Trajectory Projection: Leveraging the approximate constant-velocity property of Rectified Flow, we project the noisy state back to the data manifold to preview the image:
    \( \hat{z}_0 = z_t - t \cdot v_t \)
  • Contrastive Semantic Energy: The VLM's diagnosis is operationalized into an energy function via CLIP. This actively repels the trajectory from flawed concepts (\( e_{neg} \)) while pulling it toward the target semantic basin (\( e_{pos} \)):
    \( \mathcal{E}(\hat{z}_0) = \cos(\text{CLIP}(\hat{x}_0), e_{neg}) - \cos(\text{CLIP}(\hat{x}_0), e_{pos}) \)
  • Time-Scaled Velocity Modulation: The energy gradient is projected back to the ODE vector field. Confined by a spatial mask \( M \) from SAM3, the corrected velocity is:
    \( v_t^{corrected} = v_t + \eta t \cdot \nabla_{\hat{z}_0}\mathcal{E} \odot M \)

3. Global Feedback Loop

As a final safety mechanism, if the final image score falls below a quality threshold, a Redesign Loop is triggered. The VLM analyzes the failure mode, refines the prompt to address specific issues, and restarts the Parallel Rollout Search process, ensuring high reliability for complex queries.
